What is a Cookie?

Cookies are small text files created by a website and downloaded to your device when you access a website. These cookies are sent back to the originating website on each subsequent visit, or to any other website that recognizes the cookie. Cookies are useful because they help a website recognize a visitor's device.

Cookies perform many different tasks. The most common use is to provide login information for a user on a particular site, such as username and password, after the user clicks "Remember me on this computer," which creates a login cookie. Some cookies are used by e-commerce sites to store items placed in a shopping cart. Other cookies help you navigate between pages efficiently, remember your preferences and search settings, and generally improve your user experience. Many cookies collect information about how visitors use a website, such as which pages visitors visit most frequently. They also help ensure that online ads are more relevant to a website visitor.

How to Control and Delete Cookies through Your Browser

The ability to enable, disable, or delete cookies can be managed at the browser level. To do so, follow the instructions provided by your browser. Disabling a cookie or a category of cookies does not remove the cookie from your browser; you will need to delete it yourself from your browser.

For more information about cookies, including how to see which cookies have been set on your device, and how to manage and delete them, visit www.allaboutcookies.org.

By activating the settings on your browser that allow you to block cookies, you can refuse the setting of all or some cookies. However, if you operate your browser settings to block all cookies, you may not be able to use our Website, or specific parts of it, and some parts of our Website may not function properly.
